# Design Review: BACKEND_PLAN.md + FRONTEND_PLAN.md as one system

**Status: canonical.** This document is the single source of truth for the design review of the two companion specifications. Version 2.0, 2026-07-12; it supersedes the 2026-07-11 review, whose claims were individually re-audited against the sources (result: 44 confirmed, 7 confirmed-with-corrections, 0 refuted — see Appendix A).

**Method.** Eight independent review lenses (backend economics, backend engineering, frontend architecture, both contract directions, combined-system scalability, security, missing pieces) each read the full source documents, anchored every finding to quoted text, and recomputed all arithmetic. Findings were deduplicated (138 → 101) and adversarially verified: every critical finding survived two independent refutation attempts plus tiebreak, every high finding one; mediums/lows were batch-verified. No finding was refuted; 16 were confirmed with corrections that are incorporated below. 21 medium findings could not complete verification and are tagged **[unverified]** unless independently corroborated by the prior-review audit, in which case they are tagged **[corroborated]**.

---

## Summary verdict

These are two unusually rigorous documents that do not yet describe one system, and the backend's solvency core is now known to be wrong in one place, not merely under-specified. Where the two documents both speak, they almost always agree — event names, decimals, bounds, timing constants, and the extrinsic surface were cross-checked and match nearly everywhere. The system fails at three seams:

1. **The backend's conditional ledger has four design-level correctness defects, one of them an outright insolvency.** The VOID redemption rule pays out exactly 2× escrow by the spec's own conservation identity (B-1). The gate-market instruments and the Baseline market that the decision engine requires cannot be represented in the ledger's type system at all (B-2, B-3). And the stored conservation invariant `escrowed == branch_pairs + Σ scalar_sets` underflows on the protocol's own POL seeding flow (B-4). Solvency — the design's flagship machine-checked guarantee — is unimplementable as written.

2. **The integration contract is one-sided: the frontend's §30 "Required Parent-Document Patch" is applied 0 of 11 items.** The `FutarchyApi` runtime API (backend commits to at most 4 of 11 methods, via a one-line repo-tree comment), the `RecentCohortSummaries` ring, the `Traded`/`Observed` market events, oracle storage/event names, the WSS bootnode requirement, and the chain-identity constants all exist only in the frontend document. A team implementing BACKEND_PLAN.md as written builds a chain the canonical frontend cannot even connect to (X-1, X-4).

3. **The values-governance layer has no user-facing surface, and the headline economic-security rule is unsubstantiated.** The backend makes referenda mandatory for upgrades, oracle adjudication, and guardian elections; the frontend builds zero referenda/voting functionality (X-2). Separately, the capture-resistance rule `AttackCost ≥ 3·MEV` is asserted but no parameter scales with the value at stake — at the documents' own defaults the shortfall is a factor of ~27–290 (B-8).

Verdict: **not implementation-ready as a pair.** The ledger needs a rework, the integration contract needs a freeze, and the economic-security sizing needs to be operationalized before either §26 work breakdown starts. The good news is unchanged: both documents are honest about their unknowns, the shared factual core is solid, and most fixes are specification work, not architectural rewrites.

## Cross-cutting issues (where the two plans disagree or leave a hole between them)

**X-1 (Critical) — The integration contract exists only in the frontend document; §30 is applied 0/11.** Every patch item P-1…P-11 was checked individually against BACKEND_PLAN.md: none is applied, and the documents actively contradict each other on the indexer's role (BE §4.2/§5.2.6 "pruned to indexer … serves dashboards" vs FE P-4's event-derived-history reclassification). Grep for `FutarchyApi`, all seven uncommitted method names, `RecentCohortSummaries`, `Traded`, `Observed` returns zero hits in the backend; its entire runtime-API commitment is the §25 repo-tree comment covering at most 4 of the 11 methods, with none of the view types in §7 (X-1a). The `Traded{market, side, amount, cost, p_after}`/`Observed{market, o_t}` events that feed the frontend's whole price-history pillar are absent — pallet-market's calls table has no Events column at all (X-1b). `RecentCohortSummaries` is absent from `pallet-epoch` storage, and unlike the runtime API it has *no* fallback: cohorts, markets, and vaults are reaped after settlement, so a fresh browser can never reconstruct settlement history (X-1c; note §30 P-5/P-7 already contain the normative patch — the defect is that the backend has not applied it, and P-5 as drafted still misses the §5.2.3 storage-list edit and the `DecisionOutcomeCode` type). Consequence, corrected from the prior review: 8 of 13 frontend epics — including the entire data layer, all user-facing screens, and the release-gating FE-12 — are transitively blocked on FE-R1; FE-3/8/9/11/13 can proceed. No shippable frontend exists without a contract freeze.

**X-2 (Critical/High) — The values layer has no frontend.** The backend defines six referenda tracks with conviction voting (§16.1), makes a values `ratify` referendum mandatory for every CODE/META upgrade (§18.3(2)), routes terminal oracle disputes to an `OracleResolution` referendum (§15.2(4)), elects and recalls guardians via referenda (§20), and routes CONSTITUTIONAL-class subjects to the values track (§6.2). The frontend has zero referenda/voting/conviction/delegation surface (grep confirms) while claiming to serve "every protocol workflow." GOV holders cannot vote, ratify, adjudicate, or elect through the canonical UI; an entire governance layer is silently delegated to third-party RPC-based tooling the trust model excludes. All state involved is bounded and light-client readable, so a minimal surface is feasible — this is a scope decision, not an architectural blocker.

**X-3 (High) — Nobody owns deep history, and the frontend's replacement mechanism cannot work.** The backend prunes `ExecutionRecords` "to indexer" (§5.2.6) — an indexer the frontend declares optional. The frontend's catch-up/backfill (§15.4, §15.6) assumes `eventsAt(hash)` at arbitrary depth, but smoldot exposes only the `chainHead` group (no `archive_*` methods), events are *state*, and default peers prune state at 256 blocks ≈ 25.6 minutes. §19 E3's promise that a 2-hour gap (1,200 blocks) closes via "local-index catch-up; history continuous" is impossible against default infrastructure; opt-in backfill cannot proceed past the pinned window at all; U-3's "archive nodes are not a frontend dependency" is false for any meaningful gap.

**X-4 (High) — The browser cannot reach the chain as provisioned.** The frontend hard-requires ≥8 browser-reachable WSS bootnodes across ≥4 operators, ≥2 on port 443 (§11.3, §11.5, §28). Grep for "bootnode" in BACKEND_PLAN.md: zero hits — no node-roles row, no chain-spec requirement — and FE §30's own patch does not add one. Default collator deployments expose raw-TCP libp2p; if the chain launches per the backend spec, the canonical frontend has zero dialable peers and every workflow fails at boot.

**X-5 (High) — Trading windows disagree; the backend's forecast-trading feature is unusable and cannot even mint.** The backend keeps live-branch books trading through measurement "as a running forecast" (§11.1, §11.5) — roughly two epochs per cohort — but the frontend's pre-sign preconditions (§17.6) only construct `market.buy/sell` when the proposal is in `Trading/Extended`, and no FE screen covers forecast trading (grep: zero hits). Compounding it on the backend side: reopened books cannot function anyway, because `split_scalar` requires the vault to be `Open` and post-resolution vaults aren't — the maker cannot replenish inventory (medium, backend). The Baseline book has no FE precondition row either.

**X-6 (High) — VOID redemption is unreachable end-to-end.** Beyond the backend's 2×-payout defect (B-1), `VaultState ∈ {Open, Resolved(Branch), ScalarSettled}` has no `Void` variant, the `redeem` call requires "caller holds *winning* branch-NUM" (contradicting both-branch par refund), and the frontend's redeem preconditions demand a "winning-position balance" that does not exist under VOID. In exactly the emergency the annulment guarantee is designed for, principal recovery is blocked at the UI and self-contradictory on chain.

**X-7 (High) — Every runtime upgrade risks a ≥72-hour global signing outage that outlasts 48-hour obligations.** Corrected from the prior review (which framed this around emergency upgrades — the EMERGENCY class is playbook-only and guardians cannot install code; upgrades ride the full CODE/META market process): the real defect is descriptor lead time. The FE release train keys v(N+1) descriptors on `UpgradeAuthorized` (§12.2), but application is permissionless and can follow authorization by one block (BE §18.3(4), §30.4), while the FE release needs 2 attestations + 72h staging soak + 3-of-5 ArNS repoint (§24.4). During the resulting `ReadOnlyIncompatible` window, signing is disabled globally: 48h oracle challenge windows (28,800 blocks — recomputed, < 72h) expire unchallengeable, report windows lapse, referenda lose FE voters. Descriptors could ship *before* enactment — the artifact hash is committed at proposal queue time — but nothing requires it, and no expedited FE release path exists.

**X-8 (High) — No NUM on-ramp anywhere.** NUM (Asset-Hub USDC via reserve transfer) is the sole collateral for every user action; `pallet_xcm.reserve_transfer` is user-callable on the futarchy chain itself. The frontend has no funding/withdrawal flow, no precondition row, no screen, and no out-of-scope statement (FN-1…FN-5 don't mention funding); the inbound leg would additionally require an Asset Hub light-client connection and descriptor set that is nowhere discussed. A new user reaching the canonical frontend cannot bring collateral in; an existing user cannot exit to Asset Hub.

**X-9 (High) — Phase-3 insider risk: real funds under unfiltered sudo.** In Phase 3, users trade real NUM while a 4-of-6 founding multisig holds `pallet-sudo`, and the dangerous frame-system calls (`set_storage`, `kill_prefix`, `set_code_without_checks`…) are filtered only "post-bootstrap" — i.e., unfiltered exactly then. §22's adversary model never includes the founding multisig; the frontend renders sudo-written state as "verified" because it *is* valid finalized state. A malicious or compromised multisig can credit itself positions or destroy escrow accounting, silently breaking G-2/I-1 for real money.

**X-10 (High) — Baseline market id is undiscoverable.** FE §13.3/§14.1 read the Baseline `MarketId` "from `EpochOf`/epoch storage," but `EpochInfo { index, phase, phase_start_block }`, `CohortInfo`, and `MarketState` all lack the field — the backend's own decision engine calls `baseline_market(p.epoch)`, an accessor with no declared backing storage. Connects to B-3: the Baseline market is under-specified on both sides of the boundary.

**X-11 (Medium, batch) — Contract drift.** Each of these is small; together they are a reliable source of integration failures. (a) FE queries `Assets.Account(NUM, who)` but the backend instance is `ForeignAssets` keyed by XCM `Location`, and FE's `ChainIdentity` pins no NUM asset identifier **[corroborated]**. (b) ss58 prefix, paraId, GOV existential deposit, and the phase-flag storage the FE keys trading enablement to are specified nowhere in the backend **[corroborated]**. (c) Oracle pallet declares no storage-item names and no events, so the FE cannot enumerate its CRITICAL_SURFACE or ingest dispute history **[corroborated]**. (d) FE §15.3 misnames four epoch events (`Withdrawn` vs `ProposalWithdrawn` etc.). (e) FE re-reads "per-trade min/max params" and `MinSplit` as storage, but the backend defines them as kernel constants with "no storage representation" **[corroborated in part]**. (f) Backend T20 (emergency/VOID force-reject) emits no event, silently corrupting every event-derived archive **[unverified]**. (g) `DispatchOutcomeCode` is never defined; FE's `CohortSummary` uses `DecisionOutcomeCode` where the backend type is `DecisionOutcome` **[corroborated]**. (h) FE hardcodes the 64-position bound and other §21-tunable values. (i) FE's `execute` precondition row omits four dispatch-time checks the backend performs (ratification, meters, resource locks, gate flags) **[unverified]**. (j) Both docs cite a backend §18.6 that does not exist; more phantom refs in B-batch/F-batch below.

**X-12 (Medium, batch) — Backend workflows with no frontend surface.** Reporter registration, `recompute_proof` submission, and evidence display for oracle rounds; guardian 5-of-7 approval workflow (the system's most privileged actors have no specified signing tool); treasury stream claims and the never-rendered `nav()` view; permissionless `system.apply_authorized_upgrade` (multi-MB Wasm in-browser is architecturally nontrivial) and `welfare.snapshot` cranks — both load-bearing for liveness; bonded IncidentRegistry/MilestoneRegistry filings (welfare C/A inputs) have no UI *and* no runtime home in the backend (see B-batch). All **[unverified]** individually, but the pattern is confirmed by X-2.

**X-13 (Medium) — Unowned operational layer.** Collators, RPC/archive nodes, WSS bootnodes, monitoring, keeper subsidies beyond the metered budget, oracle evidence hosting, and all Arweave/ArNS costs have no funding line or named owner in either document; ArNS name tenure (lease vs permabuy) is undecided, and a lapsed lease lets anyone re-register the canonical name — a full T-2-class compromise with no key compromise, absent from FE §20 (see also F-batch).

**X-14 (Medium) — Transaction-fee economics is specified in neither document.** BE §5.1 promises "fees in GOV or NUM — keeps NUM-only users viable" via `pallet-asset-tx-payment`, which requires an `OnChargeAssetTransaction` GOV→NUM conversion rate; no rate source exists (no DEX, no §21 key, no oracle adapter), and the FE fee-currency selector (§17.4) builds on the same unspecified mechanism. A NUM-only user may be unable to submit any extrinsic at all — including the on-ramp itself. *(Surfaced by the completeness critic.)*

**X-15 (Medium) — Cross-repo test artifacts are uncommitted.** FE §23 gates releases on backend-published artifacts (per-release runtime wasm/metadata for descriptor regeneration, Chopsticks/Zombienet environments, chainHead fixtures); BE §24–26 never commits to publishing any of them, so the FE's primary compat control has no defined input feed. *(Surfaced by the completeness critic.)*

## BACKEND_PLAN.md — risks and open questions, by severity

### Critical

- **B-1 — VOID redemption pays 2× escrow.** §10.5 mandates that under VOID "**both** branch-NUM kinds redeem 1:1 … total payout equals E exactly," but §10.2's own identity `E = supply(AcceptNum) + Q_Accept = supply(RejectNum) + Q_Reject` makes the total payout E + E = 2E. Minimal counterexample: split 100 NUM → hold 100 AcceptNum + 100 RejectNum → redeem 200 from a 100-NUM escrow. VOID is the designated fail-static recovery for oracle deadlock (§15.2(7), PB-ORACLE-VOID), so the emergency path is insolvent by construction: first redeemers drain the vault. Any solvent VOID rule pays a single-branch holder 0.5, not par — the annulment story (PT-2) must be re-derived honestly. *(New in this review; survived two-skeptic adversarial verification.)*
- **B-2 — Gate-market instruments are unrepresentable.** §13 requires YES/NO gate complete sets per branch for CODE/META/high-TREASURY, but `PositionKind ∈ {BranchNum, Long, Short}` (§7), `VaultInfo.scalar_sets: [Balance;2]`, the single `settlement: Option<SettleScore>`, and the sole `settle_scalar(pid, s)` call cannot mint, track, or settle them; the §10.2 conservation identity has no gate term; and §5.2.1 makes the ledger "sole mint/burn authority," so no other pallet may hold these claims. Blocks three of six proposal classes and invalidates I-1 as stated.
- **B-3 — The Baseline market has no ledger home, collateral, subsidy, or settlement model.** Every decision consumes its TWAP (`r_eff = max(r_f, base − σ)`, §14.1 steps 6–7), but vaults are proposal-keyed, `PositionId` requires a `branch`, §21 has no `pol.b` row for it, and no settlement path binds an epoch-scoped book to `SettleAuthority`. Phase-3 exit criteria and §16.3's "priced second opinion" also depend on it. Undefined for every proposal in every epoch.

### High

- **B-4 — The stored solvency invariant underflows on the standard POL flow.** `escrowed == branch_pairs + Σ scalar_sets` (§5.2.1, I-1) uses a single `branch_pairs` counter, but §10.2's ops decrement per branch: POL seeding (§17.3 splits, then scalar-splits *both* branches) drives `branch_pairs` to 0 then underflows. Either every market seeding aborts with `ArithmeticOverflow` or try-state fails on a healthy vault (a spurious S1 incident). Fix: per-branch supply fields and a per-branch identity (which §10.2 already states one line later).
- **B-5 — Unpaired SHORT redemption over-pays.** `SHORT pays a − floor(a·s)` rounds *against* escrow; §10.3's proof covers only unpaired LONG. Counterexample (s = 0.70005, E = 20,000): LONG redeems 14,001, two SHORT holders redeem 3,000 each → 20,001 > 20,000. Deliberately triggerable via fragmentation; the final redeemer's extrinsic fails with up to k−1 base units stranded. Redeem unpaired SHORT at `floor(a·(1−s))` and add an atomic paired redemption.
- **B-6 — Normative LMSR test vector V1 (and V5's fee figure) is wrong.** §11.6 states `10000·ln((e^{0.1}+1)/2) = 512.925464970…`; the true value is **512.494795136…** — an error of 0.4307 NUM, ~14 orders of magnitude above the §11.4 precision bound at b = 10⁴. V5's "net −3.077552" inherits it (correct: −3.074969). V2–V4 recompute exactly. Since implementations "MUST reproduce to stated precision" and these seed the differential corpus and the frontend's TypeScript port (FE §14.5), the conformance regime is self-contradictory at launch.
- **B-7 — Trade denomination is unstated; the annulment guarantee for buyers hinges on it.** Nothing says whether `buy()` consumes NUM or branch-NUM, or whether buyers receive the mirror. If buy() takes raw NUM and the mirror stays with the book, every buyer in the losing branch loses full principal, silently voiding G-3 for the dominant user path. The pre-collateralization solvency argument also needs proceeds recycled into complete sets (recomputed: sup_x[x − ln((e^x+1)/2)] = ln 2) — the revenue-splitting mechanic is never specified and `headroom` is never sized.
- **B-8 — `AttackCost ≥ 3·MEV` is asserted, not engineered.** ADR-14/TM-1's core capture-resistance rule has no mechanism behind it: `pol.b`, `dec.v_min`, δ are flat per class; the only Ask-scaled quantity is the refundable bond. Recomputed with the doc's own A-2 flow model: a TREASURY book (L ≈ 34,657 NUM, F ≈ 17,329/day) bounds 3-day manipulation bleed at ~52k NUM against an in-cap prize of 475k NUM (at the §30.2 example NAV) to 5M (at 100M NAV) — a 27–290× shortfall. The design's central economic-security claim is unsubstantiated at its own defaults.
- **B-9 — Gate-breach "determinism" contradicts the C pillar's attested inputs.** ADR-10 claims breach facts are deterministic with "no oracle discretion," but C includes attested incident and external-price components with 48h challenge windows and up to ~15-day dispute latency — a same-day deterministic `C_daily` is impossible as specified. Either provisional attestations settle gate markets (discretion and manipulation inside the challenge window) or flags aren't daily. Split the gate: on-chain sub-components only for daily flags.
- **B-10 — GOV reflexivity leaks into C via the E component.** §12.3's E values collator/guardian bonds (GOV-denominated) using an attested GOV price, while §12.2 and §12.3's own footer exclude GOV price from W. GOV price ↑ → C ↑ → W ↑ → settlement ↑ is exactly the loop the kernel forbids, plus a 30-day-median pump vector into gate flags. Denominate security collateral in NUM within the metric.
- **B-11 — Values-ratification is self-contradictory and unwired.** §18.3(2)'s "Passed by maturity (checked at queue time and again at execute)" names two different deadlines; the §14.1 pseudocode has no ratification (or attestation-presence) check; §14.2 has no ratification row; `RejectReason` has no variant for it; the ratify track names no admissible `RuntimeCall` for its `Contains` filter. Corrected from the prior review: the timing is *not* impossible — the referendum can be submitted during Intake/Trading against the committed artifact (§30.4's worked example does exactly this); the defect is the contradictory dual deadline and the missing enforcement plumbing, not infeasibility.
- **B-12 — The normative transition table omits transitions its own diagram and economics require.** No `Rejected → Measuring` (the *most common* lifecycle path — REJECT branch trades and settles), no `FailedExecuted → Executed/Measuring` for the 72h retry; T13 (`Rerun → …, trigger decide`) conflicts with decide()'s `is_trading_or_extended()` precondition. Under "anything absent is impossible and MUST error," cohort settlement is unreachable for rejected proposals and the guardian rerun path deadlocks.
- **B-13 — Intake exhaustion is costless; preimage lifecycle unpinned.** T4 refunds the full bond when the preimage is missing, so 64 preimage-less proposals per epoch (tx fees only) exhaust the intake queue and DoS governance throughput — TM-15's "bonds price entries" is false for this path. Corrected from the prior review: the unnote-after-adoption sabotage is *recoverable* (preimages are public and `note_preimage` permissionless, so any keeper can re-note and execute within grace) — a spec-hygiene gap (no pinning discipline), not a zero-cost renege; the missing `request_preimage` discipline should still be added.
- **B-14 — Genesis economics are absent while defaults assume an 8–9 figure treasury.** No GOV supply/allocation/vesting (§27.1), no initial NUM treasury amount (§27.4), no issuance mechanism behind `iss.inflation_cap`, no collator compensation (grep: zero hits). Recomputed floors: a full 5-slot PARAM epoch needs NAV ≥ ~9.2M NUM at the 0.75% POL cap; a CODE/META slate ≥ ~69.3M. Every values-layer guarantee (A-3) is unverifiable without distribution data.
- **B-15 — Welfare normalization has no cold start.** §12.2 winsorizes against the trailing 12 finalized epochs (= 252 days) with no rule for epochs 1–12 — the window inside which *all* Phase 2–3 settlements occur. `s` is not deterministically computable for roughly the first eight months; conforming implementations can legitimately disagree.
- **B-16 — Coretime degradation and the dead-man switch compose into a wedge.** Under on-demand fallback ("chain slows, does not halt"), any inter-block gap > 8h (4,800 relay blocks) freezes the execution queue and pauses the epoch clock — and the Phase-≥4 renewal mechanism is itself a TREASURY proposal needing the frozen pipeline; each engagement also costs a 21-day proposal-free recovery epoch. The degraded state suppresses its own cure.
- **B-17 — No emergency brake and no expedited repair lane.** I-4's incident response says "freeze + investigate," but no actor can pause ledger/market calls: guardian pause is scoped to intake/queue, `pallet-safe-mode`/`tx-pause` are explicitly rejected, and playbooks don't cover it. During an active solvency exploit, split/redeem/buy/sell keep running for the entire repair cycle — best case ~25 days, worst ~43–46 days (+ up to 30 days if `code.spacing` binds) once sudo is gone. Add a narrowly-triggered PB-LEDGER-FREEZE playbook keyed to the I-4 drift flag, and an expedited CODE lane.
- **B-18 — POL subsidy vs the 0.75% NAV cap silently zeroes upgrade capacity for small treasuries.** Recomputed per-proposal commitments (2 decision + 4 gate books, ×ln2): PARAM 13,863; TREASURY>1% 55,452; CODE 103,972; META 159,424 NUM ⇒ one CODE proposal requires NAV ≥ ~13.9M NUM; five concurrent META ≥ ~106M. "Slots shrink to fit" means below ~14M NAV the chain cannot pass its own runtime upgrades — including the §29 phase-arming ones — and the refusal is silent. No minimum-viable NAV is stated anywhere; the Baseline book's unspecified `b` makes the true total unknowable (B-3).
- **B-19 — Terminal oracle adjudication rides the weakest track.** The oracle track is 50% approval / 3% support / 5-day, the lowest bar of all six tracks, yet it fixes money-settling facts for whole cohorts. A-3's protection is "supermajorities and delays" — this track has neither, and nothing disqualifies voters holding positions in the affected cohort.

### Medium (selected; each is a real spec gap)

- **Decision-grade sanity band vs gate books:** TWAP ∈ [0.02, 0.98] (§14.1 step 3) rejects every *healthy* gated proposal, whose gate books should trade near 0/1 in the doc's own examples; the V_min ambiguity swings required organic liquidity ~6×.
- **Post-resolution forecast books cannot mint:** `split_scalar` requires vault `Open`; reopened books have no inventory path (feeds X-5).
- **ProcessHold dispute censorship** (downgraded from the prior review's high after verification): on the standard schedule disputes close by ~d15–17 vs decide at d18, so the one-bond decide-window censorship *doesn't* fire; the real surface is extended proposals deciding at the epoch boundary, keeper-lag races, and the undefined interaction with IncidentRegistry/MilestoneRegistry challenge sub-games. Scope `any_open_dispute_touching` and add a threat row.
- **Slot/intake monopolization stays cheap** (arithmetic corrected): full slot capture + intake denial locks ~109k NUM of *fully refundable* bonds ≈ $314/epoch of time-value; if `MaxLiveProposals = 32` is the binding cap (it contradicts IntakeQueue = 64 — see next), monopoly drops to ~77k/$222 and pure intake denial to ~32k/$92. Governance throughput is censorable for pocket change; TM-15 lacks the row.
- **IntakeQueue = 64 vs `MaxLiveProposals = 32` "(all states)":** mutually unsatisfiable; every PoV/storage budget derived from 32 (Vaults, `MaxLiveMarkets = 32·7+1 = 225`) is at stake. Related: the live-market bound is stated as ≤121, ≈225, and 6-vs-7 books/proposal in different places.
- **Positions map is globally unbounded and under-priced** (0.01 NUM/entry; reaping needs a full-map scan since the key order is `(AccountId, PositionId)` — per-vault reap has no prefix to drain **[corroborated]**); `MaxPositionsPerAccount = 64` conflicts with POL seeding itself.
- **`ProposalClass::Emergency` has no lifecycle** — no bond, markets, state-machine path, or decision rule; the ADR-3 classifier cannot satisfy its completeness obligation.
- **`epoch.length` is META-amendable but the phase schedule is fixed absolute offsets** — no scaling rule, no in-flight-cohort rule.
- **SettleAuthority wiring is contradictory** (welfare/oracle owns it; pallet-epoch's `settle_cohort` drives settlement and can't call it under §6.1's one-origin-per-path rule).
- **Guardian `force_rerun` has no state-machine integration** — undefined book/position/TWAP handling for one of five enumerated guardian powers.
- **EmergencyPlaybook's admissible call set isn't enumerated in §6.2** (reframed after verification: kernel prohibitions *are* independently enforceable via §6.3(b) + per-pallet EnsureOrigin; this is an under-specification of the capability table, not an open privilege hole).
- **IncidentRegistry / MilestoneRegistry have no owning pallet** — a bonded filing/challenge/slashing subsystem feeding the C pillar is unassigned, unbounded, and unbudgeted.
- **Oracle bonds are flat constants, not value-at-stake-scaled:** for a ~1.2M-NUM META cohort, shifting s by 0.10 on a subjective attested component nets ~+50k NUM even after forfeiting the full 70k bond stack **[unverified]**.

## FRONTEND_PLAN.md — risks and open questions, by severity

### High

- **F-1 — Historical reads at depth cannot work as designed.** The §15.4 ingest loop and §15.6 backfill assume `eventsAt(hash)` at arbitrary depth through smoldot, which exposes only `chainHead` (pinned blocks; no `archive_*`); events are state, pruned at ~256 blocks by default peers.
